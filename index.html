About

Lebron James is widely considered as one of the greatest basketball players of all time. With his awe-inspiring skills, unmatched athleticism, and fierce competitiveness, he has dominated the court and made a lasting impact on the game. Throughout his illustrious career, James has amassed numerous accolades, including four NBA championships, four MVP awards, and 17 All-Star selections. This website is dedicated to providing comprehensive information about the life, career, and achievements of LeBron James, along with interesting insights and trivia about one of the greatest players in the history of the sport. Whether you're a fan or simply want to learn more about this legendary figure, you've come to the right place.

Lebron James Career

Lebron James was selected by the Cleveland Cavaliers with the first overall pick in the 2003 NBA draft. Named the 2004 NBA Rookie of the Year, winning the NBA MVP award in 2009 and 2010 and leading the Cavaliers to their first NBA Finals appearance in 2007.

Cleveland Cavaliers 2003-2010

After failing to win a championship with Cleveland, Lebron James left in 2010 as a free agent to join the Miami Heat. Lebron James won his first two NBA championships while playing for the Heat in 2012 and 2013; in both of these years, he also earned the league's MVP and Finals MVP awards.

Miami Heat 2010-2014

In 2014, James re-signed with the Cavaliers. A month after signing, the Cavaliers acquired Love from the Timberwolves, forming a new star trio along Irving. Cleveland Cavaliers 2014-2018

In 2018, James signed with the Lakers, where he won the 2020 NBA championship and his fourth Finals MVP. On August 18, 2022, James re-signed with the Los Angeles Lakers on a two-year, $97.1 million contract. The contract extension made James the highest-paid athlete in NBA history at $528.9 million, surpassing Kevin Durant in all-time earnings.
